duplicated_service_responsibility | Notification Core Service (platform) vs Notification Connector Service (integrations) vs notification_service (service map) | Notification ownership is split three ways: core dispatch in platform, external channel wiring in integrations, and full transactional notification management in a standalone service. This creates unclear ownership for templates, delivery state, retries, and provider failover. | high | Define a single Notification domain/service as source of truth (templates, message lifecycle, receipts). Keep provider adapters as integration plugins owned by that service, not separate domain ownership.
domain_overlap | organization.Enrollment & Membership Service vs learning.Enrollment Orchestration Service | Both domains claim enrollment lifecycle responsibilities; organization focuses membership while learning handles course/path enrollment. Boundary ambiguity can cause duplicated enrollment state and conflicting workflows. | high | Split explicitly: organization owns org/team membership only; learning owns course/path enrollment only. Rename services to `Membership Service` and `Learning Enrollment Service` and forbid cross-domain writes.
domain_overlap | content.Catalog & Discovery Service vs learning.Course Lifecycle Service vs course_catalog_service (service map) | Catalog/discoverability appears in content domain, while course metadata/catalog listing is owned by course_catalog_service in the service map. Course publish-state and discoverability can be mutated from multiple places. | high | Move canonical course catalog metadata ownership to one service (either Course Lifecycle or Catalog Service). Other domains should consume read models/events only.
incorrect_grouping | organization_catalog_service (service map) | `organization_catalog_service` includes learner group definitions used for targeting/access policy, which overlaps identity/access concerns and organization structure concerns. It is currently too broad for a single bounded context. | medium | Decompose into `Organization Structure Service` (BU/team/location) and `Audience Segmentation Service` (learner groups for targeting), with clear upstream/downstream contracts.
duplicated_service_responsibility | identity domain service set: Auth Service + Session/Token Service + SSO Federation Service + auth_identity_service | Authentication and token lifecycle are represented as both consolidated (`auth_identity_service`) and split services (Auth, Session/Token, SSO). This suggests duplicate implementations or unclear target architecture. | medium | Choose one operating model: (a) consolidated Identity service with internal modules, or (b) separately deployable auth/token/federation services with strict API boundaries; document the decision in domain map.
domain_overlap | analytics.Event Ingestion Service vs platform.Audit & Logging Service | Both can become event sinks for operational/user events. Without a separation, product events may be duplicated or inconsistently routed between analytics and audit logs. | medium | Define event classes: Audit events (immutable compliance trail) owned by platform; Product/learning telemetry owned by analytics. Use a shared event bus with separate schemas/retention policies.
incorrect_grouping | Policy/Compliance Assignment Service under organization domain | Compliance policy assignment is placed under organization, while training completion/compliance evidence is consumed in learning/analytics/event flows. This can couple org hierarchy changes to compliance rules incorrectly. | medium | Move policy definition/assignment to a dedicated Compliance domain (or learning governance subdomain). Organization should only provide hierarchy attributes used for policy targeting.
domain_overlap | assessment domain (Grading & Feedback Service) vs learning.Progress Tracking Service | Assessment scoring and learning progress/completion both compute learner outcomes. If completion criteria are recalculated in both domains, results diverge (e.g., pass/fail vs completion status). | medium | Make assessment own raw attempts/scores and emit outcome events; learning progress should derive completion from those events via deterministic rules, not duplicate grading logic.
